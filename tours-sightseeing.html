Tours & Sightseeing

Let Braendle Limousine welcome you to a world of luxury tailormade travel. Our dedicated in house specialists unearth amazing destinations and curate bespoke travel experiences. We offer a number of pre-planned tours or will be delighted to create something that suits your personal desires, timeframe and budget.

Please call +41 76 517 77 54 to find out more or create a bespoke limousine tour in Switzerland and the surrounding areas.

Our most popular tours:

Tour 1: Basel City & Museum Tour

Fancy a tour through Basel’s old town with its 15 th century architecture, a romantic picnic in one of the city’s beautiful green spaces or a refreshing jump into the turquoise waters of the Rhine? All of this and more is possible when you book an exclusive a Braendle tour of the area. Or if you or your companions are art lovers supplement your visit with a stop at one of the cities more than 35 museums to enjoy a range of stunning art. In the Basel City Tour we will be delighted to give you an overview of the important architecture in the area.

The city is considered a mecca for architecture aficionados with buildings by famous architects greeting the visitor at every turn. Fondation Beyeler by Renzo Piano, Herzog & de Meuron’s Schaulager or the banking building by Mario Botta. For Art lovers we recommend the Braendle Basel Museum Tour An easy and relaxing way to explore some of the most important artworks housed within the region.

The Basel Museum Tour includes:

  • Fondation Bayeler Riehen
  • Fine Arts Museum Basel
  • Schaulager, Museum Tinquely Basel
  • Museum of Contemporary Art
  • Kunsthalle Basel
  • Vitra Design Museum, Germany (10 minutes ride from Basel)

Tour 2: Lucerne & Zurich

Surrounded by towering snow-capped mountains and lush green valleys, Lucerne looks like an old postcard brought to life. This beautiful Swiss town is filled with preserved medieval towers, small balconies under charming rooftops, old wooden bridges, and gorgeous terraces and walkaways lining Lake Lucerne, with elegant swans and numerous ducks gliding across the tranquil turquoise waters.

Zurich, too, is picture perfect with its iconic lake and stunning buildings offering views for keen photographers and happy snappers alike. Located in the heart of Europe, this cosmopolitan metropolis offers all the modern amenities without sacrificing any of its natural beauty. In fact, for years the city has ranked among the world’s top in terms of quality of life.

These beautiful Swiss gems truly have to be seen to be believed.

Tour 3: Schaffhausen Rhine falls

Travel with the Braendle team to Europe’s biggest waterfall, close to the German border in Switzerland. The Rhine Falls provides a magnificent spectacle as it cascades over rocks just south of Schaffhausen. 600 m³ (21,189 cubic feet) of water plunges over the falls in the summer, across a width of 150 meters (492 feet) and a height of 23 meters (75.5 feet).

Join us to walk the adventure path and take the elevator from Laufen Castle to the panorama platforms offering an unobstructed view of the rushing water. Take a boat ride to the Rhine Falls basin, the rocks in the middle of the falls, downstream to the national border or further to the Rheinau power plant.

The choice is yours and we’ll be happy to help you to get the best from this wonderful area.

Tour 4: Basel - Lucerne – Bürgenstock

You will start your day with a scenic Braendle Limousine journey through beautiful countryside along the shores of the Lake of the Four Cantons to the charming city of Lucerne. Once you reach the water, the newly designed Bürgenstock shuttle boat will take you to Kehrsiten on a scenic cruise before you board the Bürgenstock Bahn, a legendary funicular railway built in 1888 as a tribute to the art and technology of the belle époque.

Once at the top of Mt. Bürgenstock you’ll find a great place for hiking. The rock-face path “Felsenweg”, completed in 1905 is a traditional, some say mythical, mountain trail that leads to the towering 152.8 metre-tall Hammetschwand Lift. The Bürgenstock Resort nestles in the heart of Central Switzerland atop a spectacular mountain ridge offering incomparable views of Lake Lucerne. This spa town resort has been a popular holiday destination for the wealthy since 1872 and is a beautiful place to spend some time.

Tour 5: Basel – Lugano - Locarno/ Ascona

Leave Basel in the comfort of your limousine and enjoy the sunny Swiss sights as you head across the country to Lugano and the Lake Maggiore area with its stunning villas and Italian influenced architecture. Before relaxing on the shores of the largest lake in southern Switzerland we recommend you include a stop in the ancient village of Ascona on the northern shore.

Known for its Latin ambience and mild climate, Ascona has proved itself to be the perfect location for many successful cultural events such as JazzAscona and Switzerland’s most important film festival.

Tour 6: Geneva (via Bern – Gruyere – Lausanne)

Head to French-speaking Geneva in confidence that our multilingual chauffeurs speak the language and know this stunning city very well.

Laying idyllically between Alpine peaks and vineyards, by the largest lake in Western Europe, this city on the westernmost edge of Switzerland has much to offer culturally. And we always recommend your first stop should be to take in the magnificent view of the lake before soaking up a bit of Geneva city life.

Next up, why not take a rewarding excursion to Mont Saleve, situated in neighboring France. The cableway lifts visitors to an altitude of 1,100 metres in less than five minutes, giving rise to outstanding vistas across the city of Geneva, Lake Geneva, the chain of Alps, the Jura and Montblanc.

Tour 7: Colmar and Alsace, the charming French region next to Switzerland

Let Braendle drive you along the French wine road in style, taking an enchanting route through hilly vineyards and flower-filled villages, to Colmar, famed for its stunning colourful houses along the banks of the Lauch river.

Take a guided tour through the beautiful historic town centre, enjoy some time in Little Venice an enjoy lunch in a typical Alsatian restaurant or a simple “Winstub”, for an experience befitting this wonderful region. On the way back, we’ll drive to the medieval towns of Riquewihr and Eguisheim, at the foot of the Vosges, and pay a visit to a traditional winery – a crucial part of any visit to the Alsace.
